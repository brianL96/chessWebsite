General Rules & Terminology:

  • Every chess variation on this site is a two-player game.
  • After connecting to a room, the player is assigned either white or black pieces. The player with white pieces always goes first.
  • In subsequent rematches players will switch sides.
  • A turn is completed after a player has moved a piece.
  • A move is completed after both players have completed a turn.
  • A match is considered in progress when the timer for white begins to countdown. In the first match, white's timer will countdown only after an initial move is completed. In subsequent rematches, white's timer will start right away.
  • A match is considered not in progress before white's timer starts, and after the match has concluded.
  • A forfeit occurs when a player leaves a match that is in progress.
  • A player can only be in a single match for a particular game mode (Classic, King Of The Hill, etc.) at a time.
  • However, a player can be in multiple matches at the same time, as long as each match is in a different game mode.
  • If a player tries to find a new match within a game mode they're currently playing a match in, they'll first be disconnected from their current match, which may lead to a forfeit.

    • Winning and Losing a Match:

  • If a player is put in checkmate, their opponent wins.
  • If a player resigns, their opponent wins.
  • If a player's timer hits 00:00, and their opponent still has sufficient pieces to checkmate them, their opponent wins.
  • If a player forfeits, their opponent wins. Any disconnect from a game in progress, other than an admin forcing a disconnect, will result in a forfeit.
  • In King Of The Hill, the player that gets their king on one of the golden squares wins.

    • Matches Concluding In A Draw:

  • Once per turn either player can offer a draw. The opposing player can accept the draw, or reject the offer by moving a piece.
  • If a player's timer hits 00:00, and their opponent has insufficient pieces to checkmate them, the match is declared a draw.
  • During a player's turn, if their King is not in check and there's no legal moves available to them, the game ends in stalemate.

    • Other Draw Conditions

  • Matches will be declared a draw if any of the conditions below are met. If more than one condition is met, the result reported follows the order they're listed here:
  • Both players lack sufficient pieces to checkmate their opponent:
  • Both players have only their kings.
  • One player has only their king, and the other player has only their king plus either a single knight or a single bishop.
  • Both players have only their king plus either a single knight or a single bishop.
  • The Fifty Move Rule: 50 consecutive moves (100 turns) have passed without either a pawn being moved, or a piece being captured.
  • Threefold Repetition: occurs when either player encounters the same board 3 times:
  • All pieces on the board occupy the same spots,
  • All legal moves to the player whose turn it is are the same,
  • All remaining castling rights are the same.
  • If a particular pawn capture via en passant is availiable in one board, but not in another, then the player is not encountering the same board.
  • If a player castles either left or right, or they move their king outside of castling, then they've lost both their castling rights. If a player moves their left or right rook, they've lost the right to castle in that direction (if they still had the right). A player does not lose their right to castle, in either direction, if the corresponding rook is captured (even though castling in that direction has become impossible). Note: if a particular game mode has the player starting with no rooks on their side, they're given no castling rights.
  • Only the previous 15 boards of a match will be examined for Threefold Repetition.

    • Getting Disconnected From A Room:

  • If either player leaves a match that's in progress, both players are disconnected.
  • If a player connects to a room and an opponent doesn't show up within a set time, they'll be disconnected.
  • During the first match, if a room is fully occupied and the first move isn't completed within a set time, both players are disconnected.
  • During the first match, if the player with white pieces completes a turn and then disconnects before a move is completed, any remaining player is disconnected.
  • After a match is completed, if a rematch is not agreed upon within a set time, or either player leaves before a rematch is agreed upon, any remaining player is disconnected.

    • Saving and Replaying Games:

  • A player can save a match at any point, after at least one turn has passed. The current status, or the result, of the match is reported. Saved games can be replayed and edited, and those edits can be saved.
  • Currently, if a match has over 100 moves (200 turns), that match will not be saved.
  • Currently, an account can have maximally 20 saved games at a time.

    • ELO:

  • Players have a seperate ELO for each game mode. The starting ELO is 300.
  • Players gain ELO when they win a match, and lose ELO when they lose a match. The amount of ELO exchanged depends on the initial difference between ELO scores, and whether the player with initially higher ELO, or intially lower ELO, won. Draws and stalmates result in no ELO exchange.
  • Players are ranked by ELO in the Leaderboards.
